Open the console in browser to see the result of the code

CHROME:

Press either CTRL + SHIFT + J to open the “console” tab of the Developer Tools. Alternative method: Press either CTRL + SHIFT + I or F12 to open the Developer Tools. Press ESC (or click on “Show console” in the bottom right corner) to slide the console up. Note: In Chrome’s dev tools, there is a “console” tab. However, a smaller “slide-up” console can be opened while any of the other tabs is active.

SAFARI:

Press CTRL + ALT + I to open the Web Inspector. See Chrome’s step 2. (Chrome and Safari have pretty much identical dev tools.) Note: Step 1 only works if the “Show Develop menu in menu bar” check box in the Advanced tab of the Preferences menu is checked!

vIE9:

Press F12 to open the developer tools. Click the “console” tab.

FIREFOX:

Press CTRL + SHIFT + K to open the Web console (COMMAND + SHIFT + K on Macs). or, if Firebug is installed (recommended): Press F12 to open Firebug. Click on the “console” tab.

OPERA:

Press CTRL + SHIFT + I to open Dragonfly. Click on the “console” tab.
